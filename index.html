Media are the collective outlets or means of communication that is used to deliver or transfer information to a large sum of people. It is the main medium for mass communication and are often seen everywhere.

Media has several forms that has gone through the different eras. The use of media has been observed even before the existence of Christ in different forms that are far from the usual use of media that we see today.

These eras are categorized into four timelines namely: Pre-historic Era, Ancient Era, Industrial Era, and the Electronic Era.

PRE-HISTORIC ERA

200,000 BCE- 4,000 BCE

ANCIENT ERA

3,000 BCE- 100 CE

INDUSTRIAL ERA

1140 - 1890

ELECTRONIC ERA

1906- PRESENT

PRE-HISTORIC ERA

The prehistoric inhabitants used stone tools and caves as medium for their artworks and as a way to pass time. This is the form of media that is the most used in communicating.

ANCIENT ERA

In this era, writing systems like cuneiform and hieroglyphics were used to record information.

INDUSTRIAL ERA

This period brought in economic and societal changes, such as the use of developed machine tools and the steam engine in printing and transfering media.

ELECTRONIC ERA

The electronic age started when people utilized the power of electricity that made electronic devices like transistor radio and television work.
